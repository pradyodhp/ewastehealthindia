Key issues to discuss
👶
Child labour
In informal e-waste zones like Seelampur, children as young as 6 work alongside adults. They manually dismantle circuit boards, burn cables, and handle acid baths — with no protection and no education. Their developing bodies are far more vulnerable to lead and mercury poisoning.
~35% of workers are minors
🧤
Lack of PPE
Personal Protective Equipment — gloves, N95 masks, protective suits, goggles — is almost entirely absent in the informal sector. Workers perform acid leaching with bare hands. The cost of PPE (~₹500/month) is cited as the barrier, yet the lifetime medical costs of toxic exposure run into lakhs.
Less than 4% PPE usage
💨
Air quality exposure
Open burning of cables and acid leaching in open air releases dioxins, furans, lead particles, and brominated flame retardants directly into the surrounding neighbourhood. PM2.5 levels in e-waste zones are 15–22× the WHO safe limit. Residents within 1km radius are also affected.
PM2.5 up to 340 µg/m³
